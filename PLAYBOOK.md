# Ethereum Community Operating System — Global Playbook

## The Model: SF → Singapore → New York → Everywhere

This isn't just Ethereum SF. It's a replicable community operating system that can run in any city with an Ethereum community. Start with the three hottest nodes:

1. **San Francisco** — the home base, where the model gets proven
2. **Singapore** — deep OG community (ESG has been running for 12+ years)
3. **New York** — Colin has existing relationships and has done this before

Once the playbook works in three cities, any community leader anywhere can pick it up and run it locally.

---

## What Each City Needs

- A decentralized event calendar
- A local business alliance (annual memberships from local companies)
- A committed group of verified community members
- Weekly formats: Demo Day, company updates, vibe coding nights
- Recording/archiving infrastructure
- A bounty system for community tasks

That's it. No EF grant required. No single organizer burning out.

---

## Vibe Coding Nights — The Gateway Event

The lowest-barrier, highest-impact recurring event format.

### What You Need
- A space (office, coworking, venue — anyone can host)
- Coffee
- AI credits (or just free models)
- Someone to show people how to get started

### Available Credits & Resources (SF)
- **Nebius** — $100 coupons to give out to attendees
- **Cline** — credits available through Frontier Tower
- **OpenRouter** — credits committed (used at Denver event)
- **Anthropic/Claude** — credits available
- **Free tier** — OpenRouter has tons of free models, Nvidia has free models

### The Formula: Coffee, Code, Credits

Show people how to use all the free and open source models. Hand out credits to get them building. No gatekeeping, no advanced knowledge required. Vibe coding is the most community-oriented format because all you need is permission to start.

### Why This Matters for Ethereum
- Builders learn to ship faster
- Open source AI aligns with Ethereum's open source ethos
- New contributors get productive on day one
- Every city can run this — the credits are the funding

## The Bounty System

Members of the alliance can post bounties for community tasks:

- **Testing** — Got a new project? Post a bounty, get verified community members to test it
- **Content** — Need a writeup, video, or tutorial? Bounty it
- **Events** — Need someone to run AV at a meetup? Bounty it
- **Outreach** — Want to bring new people into a specific initiative? Bounty it
- **Development** — Need a feature built, a bug fixed, a prototype? Bounty it

### How It Works
- Member companies fund bounties
- Verified Ethereum SF (or Singapore, or NY) community members can pick them up
- Builds a reputation system — people who complete bounties consistently become known and trusted
- Companies get work done, community members earn, everyone stays engaged

### Why Bounties > Sponsorship
When you sponsor an event, you get a logo. When you post a bounty, you get work done AND community engagement. The bounty system turns marketing spend into actual ecosystem development.

---

## Verified Community Members

A lightweight reputation layer across cities:

- Verified members are people known to the local community — they show up, they contribute, they're trusted
- Not a token gate or an exclusive club — just a way to know who's real
- Protects against scams (Telegram group scanners, fake accounts, etc.)
- Verified members can: pick up bounties, host events, access the "In Town" board, open their office for community use
- Verification comes through contribution, not payment

---

## The Cross-City Network

If you're a verified member in SF and you travel to Singapore:
- You show up on the "In Town" board
- Local verified members can connect with you
- You can pick up local bounties
- You're trusted because the network vouches for you

This is how you keep a transient community together. The people who travel to every Devcon, every ETH Denver, every regional event — they're not strangers anymore. They're verified members of a global network.

## Learning from the OGs

### Engineers.SG — The Blueprint

[Engineers.SG](https://engineers.sg) is the direct inspiration for this model. A not-for-profit community initiative that has documented Singapore's tech scene for 12+ years. They solved this problem first:

- **Engineers.SG** — searchable video archive of every recorded meetup and talk
- **WeBuild.SG** — decentralized events calendar for the Singapore tech community
- **KopiJS** — casual coffee meetups (the original "coffee, code, credits")
- **GeekBrunch.SG** — social gatherings

They don't own every event. They show up as the recording/production partner for existing community meetups. Everything is documented and discoverable. They sustain on donations and partnerships — lean, reliable, no drama.

Run by Code Craft Pte Ltd, the Engineers.SG team has earned universal respect by never taking credit, always showing up, and serving the community for over a decade. This is the gold standard.

### What Ethereum SF Adds to the Engineers.SG Model

| Engineers.SG | Ethereum SF |
|---|---|
| Donation/Patreon funded | Annual membership alliance ($10K/year) |
| All tech communities | Ethereum-specific with clear boundary |
| Video archive | Video archive + livestreaming |
| WeBuild.SG calendar | Decentralized calendar + "In Town" board |
| KopiJS coffee meetups | Vibe coding nights (coffee + code + AI credits) |
| Reputation through presence | Verified members + bounty system |
| Singapore only | Multi-city network (SF → Singapore → NY → everywhere) |

The foundation is the same. The Ethereum version adds sustainable funding, cultural protection, and cross-city networking.

**The open source tradition** — This model comes from a long tradition of serving the software community. From the early days of Microsoft trying to sue open source, to today's Ethereum ecosystem. The thread is the same: give back, serve first, build trust over time.

**What's different about crypto** — Giving back is actually encouraged and can be funded on-chain. Bounties, grants, DAOs — the infrastructure for community funding exists. The challenge has always been organizing it at the local level. That's what this playbook solves.

## Why This Works

Most "community operating systems" are designed by people who've never actually run a community long-term. This one is built from 10+ years of doing it — the burnout, the politics, the bull market opportunists, the bear market loyalty.

The insight: you don't need a complex platform. You need:
1. A calendar (so people know what's happening)
2. A membership model (so it's funded)
3. A bounty system (so people stay engaged)
4. Verified members (so you know who's real)
5. Recording/archiving (so nothing is lost)
6. A clear boundary around Ethereum (so the culture is protected)

Coffee, code, and credits. That's the foundation.
