# Ethereum SF — Membership & Sponsorship Model

## The SF Ethereum Alliance

Ethereum SF operates a local business association for companies in the SF Ethereum ecosystem.

### Annual Membership — $10K/year

**What you get:**
- Featured at every Ethereum SF event for the entire year — automatically, no asking
- Access to the weekly SF company update — share launches, news, hiring, anything you want the community to know
- Demo day stage time for your team
- Your company recorded and archived as part of the SF Ethereum ecosystem
- Community recognition as a supporter of the local ecosystem

**Membership criteria — you must have at least one of:**
- An office in SF
- A founder based in SF
- A team based in SF

This is a local alliance. You're in because you're here.

### What This Solves

When individual people sponsor events out of pocket, they end up carrying all the operational burden — food, design, AV, logistics. The annual membership model distributes that cost across the companies who benefit from the community. No one has to stress about whether there's a budget for pizza. The money is there, the events are covered, the community is supported year-round.

No more "I'll throw in $200 but I want my logo on everything." The membership covers it. Everyone is featured equally as part of the SF community.

## Weekly Formats

### Company Update
All member companies can share updates with the community each week — product launches, open roles, milestones, things they're working on. This replaces the need to sponsor a one-off event just to get the word out.

### Decentralized Demo Day (DDD)
A recurring (potentially weekly) demo day where anyone can showcase what they're building. This is the heart of Ethereum SF — builders showing work, getting feedback, iterating in public.

## The Ethereum Line — L1/L2 Policy

This is the most important boundary Ethereum SF sets.

### Ethereum = Community

The Ethereum SF calendar, events, and community spaces are for the Ethereum ecosystem. This includes:
- Ethereum L1
- Ethereum L2s and rollups
- EVM-compatible chains (with nuance)
- Projects building on or contributing to Ethereum infrastructure
- Cross-cutting topics like ZK, privacy, and cryptography (when presented in an ecosystem-neutral or Ethereum-aligned way)

These events are community-driven. The membership model above applies.

### Non-Ethereum Crypto = Business

L1s outside the Ethereum ecosystem regularly approach with offers ($10K+/month to host events). These are welcome — but on completely separate terms:

- **Minimum $20K** — this is a business arrangement, not a community thing
- **Clearly labeled** — it is NOT an Ethereum SF event. Separate branding, separate communication, separate chat
- **Transparency** — attendees must know what the event is. No hiding an L1 pitch inside an "Ethereum meetup"
- **Separate channel** — people interested in general crypto/L1 topics can opt into a separate chat or track

### Why This Matters

The Ethereum community gets diluted when every chain with a marketing budget can buy their way onto the stage. Colin's rule from working at an L1 in 2018: "Here's Bitcoin, here's Ethereum, here's my thing." Be transparent. Don't conflate.

If all goes well, the membership model and clear boundaries protect the community from capture while still allowing business relationships with non-Ethereum chains on honest terms.

## Go-to-Market: Letter of Intent

There are at least 100 crypto companies in SF with $10K in marketing budget. The play:

1. **Start with LOIs, not payments** — Ask companies to sign a letter of intent committing to the annual membership. No money upfront.
2. **Build the coalition** — Once companies see others signing on, momentum builds. Nobody wants to be the one SF Ethereum company not on the list.
3. **Collect when critical mass hits** — Once enough LOIs are in, convert to paid memberships and fund the full operation.

### The Math

| Members | Annual Budget |
|---------|--------------|
| 25 | $250K |
| 50 | $500K |
| 100 | $1M |

At scale, $1M/year funds:
- Professional AV and livestreaming for every event
- Full-time operations support
- Food and venue costs covered year-round
- Website, calendar, and archive infrastructure
- Staff to run events so no single organizer burns out
- Packaging the model for other cities

This turns Ethereum SF from a volunteer effort into a self-sustaining community institution — funded entirely by the local ecosystem, no EF grants needed.

## Packaging the Model

Once this substrate is strong in SF, it can be packaged and shared with other city communities. Instead of every local Ethereum group going to EF for a $50K grant, they can:

1. Establish a local business alliance with annual memberships
2. Use the Ethereum SF playbook for event guidelines, calendar, and recordings
3. Self-sustain through their local ecosystem

This is how the secretariat model scales — each city has the tools and framework to support itself.
