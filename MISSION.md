# Ethereum SF

## Mission Statement

Ethereum SF is a decentralized calendar and community hub for San Francisco's Ethereum ecosystem. We connect builders, OGs, newcomers, and curious minds through open events — focused on real work, real conversation, and genuine human connection. No politics, no sponsor-driven agendas, just community.

Dogs welcome.

---

## Core Values

- **Community over commerce** — Sponsors are appreciated, never the headline. Events exist to serve attendees, not logos.
- **Builders get the mic** — Every event should carve out time for people to demo projects, share works-in-progress, and get real feedback.
- **Decentralized by default** — No single organizer owns the calendar. Communities, companies, and individuals all contribute.
- **Open doors** — Events should be accessible and welcoming to newcomers, not just insiders.
- **Protect the culture** — Set boundaries that keep the community healthy through bull and bear cycles. Reward people who show up consistently, not just when it's profitable.
- **Serve before you lead** — If you're new to Ethereum SF, start by contributing. Volunteer, help run events, support the people already here. Earn trust through service, not self-promotion.

## The Calendar

The calendar is open. Anyone can submit an event — no venue approval required.

**To be listed, your event should:**

1. **Be relevant** — Cover Ethereum or adjacent decentralized ecosystem topics.
2. **Be community-first** — The primary purpose is education, building, or connection — not a product launch or sales pitch.
3. **Give builders stage time** — Include a demo slot, lightning talks, or open mic for attendees to share their work.
4. **Keep sponsorship in its place** — Sponsors may be acknowledged, but should not dominate the agenda, naming, or content.
5. **Be open to the community** — Free or low-cost attendance preferred. No gated, invite-only corporate events.

Submissions are reviewed by community stewards to ensure alignment with these guidelines.

## "In Town" Board

Traveling to SF? Post that you're around. Heading to New York, Denver, Devcon, or anywhere else? Drop a note.

No pressure, no obligation — just a way for community members to find each other across cities. The Ethereum community is global but relationships are local. This helps bridge the gap between OGs, locals, and people passing through.

## Livestreaming & Archive

Every event should be recorded and published. Crypto events happen constantly in SF and there's no record of most of them. By livestreaming and archiving, we build:

- A searchable database of talks, demos, and discussions
- A resource for people who can't attend in person
- A living history of what this community is building

## Who's Here

The SF Ethereum community has layers:

- **OGs** — The people who've been here since the early meetups, who quit their jobs and went all in. They carry the culture.
- **Locals** — Consistent builders and contributors who show up in bull and bear markets.
- **Travelers** — People passing through SF for events, conferences, or work. Welcome — use the "In Town" board.
- **Newcomers** — Curious people from SF's broader tech scene, institutional folks, non-crypto natives. We need to bridge them in, show them Ethereum is different from the meme coin noise.

Everyone has a place. But the culture is set by the people who stay and serve.

---

## On Sponsorship & Sustainability

Sponsors help cover food, AV, and space. That's valuable. But:

- Sponsors don't buy the agenda
- Sponsors don't get to turn a community event into a product demo
- Food is the great equalizer — pizza, drinks, something to bond over
- Keep budgets lean and stress low

The goal is events that are successful without anyone being burned out. If the organizer is stressed about politics, sponsors, and logistics all at once, something is wrong. Distribute the work. Keep it simple.

---

## Bridging the Gap

San Francisco still has an anti-crypto bias in parts of the tech scene. Ethereum SF should be a bridge:

- Show institutional and traditional tech people that Ethereum is fundamentally different from speculative noise
- Make events approachable — not every event needs "Ethereum" in the title (see: Nerd Compliments and similar community-first formats)
- Let the work speak for itself

---

## The Vision

This model — decentralized calendar, community-first events, open archives, cross-city networking — should work everywhere. What starts in SF can expand to any city with an Ethereum community that cares about doing it right.

The goal isn't to control. It's to create a framework that protects sincere builders, welcomes newcomers, and keeps the community together through every cycle.

### Inspired by Engineers.SG

[Engineers.SG](https://engineers.sg) has documented Singapore's tech community for 12+ years — recording meetups, maintaining a decentralized events calendar (WeBuild.SG), and hosting casual gatherings (KopiJS). They proved this model works through consistent, humble service. Ethereum SF builds on their blueprint with sustainable funding, Ethereum-specific cultural boundaries, and a cross-city network.
